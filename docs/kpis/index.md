# KPIs

**Track business objectives with measurable KPIs linked to your metrics.**

KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) help you monitor progress toward your organization's goals by combining your data metrics with targets and status tracking. Each KPI displays its current value, target, and visual status indicator (green, amber, or red) based on your configured thresholds.

## What are KPIs?

KPIs transform raw metrics into actionable insights by adding:
- **Targets**: Set specific goals you want to achieve
- **Direction**: Define whether higher or lower values are better
- **Status thresholds**: Configure green/amber/red ranges
- **Time tracking**: Monitor progress over different time periods
- **Visual charts**: See trends and patterns at a glance

## Getting started

Your KPI dashboard shows all your organization's key indicators in a card-based layout. Each card displays:
- Current metric value
- Target value
- RAG (Red/Amber/Green) status indicator
- Trend chart showing historical performance
- Percentage change from the previous period

## Creating your first KPI

1. **Select New KPI** from the dashboard
2. **Choose a metric** from your metrics library or create one inline
3. **Set your target** value and whether higher or lower is better
4. **Configure status thresholds** for green, amber, and red ranges
5. **Choose time grain** (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly)
6. **Add program tags** to organize KPIs by project or initiative

## Managing KPIs

Use the search and filter controls to find specific KPIs:
- **Search**: Find KPIs by name
- **Type filter**: Filter by metric type (count, sum, average, etc.)
- **Program filter**: Show KPIs for specific programs or projects
- **Status filter**: View only KPIs that are on track, need attention, or off track

Click any KPI card to view detailed information, annotations, and historical data in the detail drawer.
