# Annotations

**Add contextual notes and beneficiary quotes to explain KPI changes and provide rich context for your performance data.**

Annotations help you capture the story behind your KPI data by documenting why values changed, what interventions were implemented, and how beneficiaries responded to your programs.

## Types of annotations

### Notes
General contextual information about KPI performance:
- **Explanation of changes**: Why did this metric increase or decrease?
- **External factors**: Seasonal effects, policy changes, external events
- **Program interventions**: New initiatives, staff changes, process improvements
- **Data quality issues**: Known problems with data collection or processing

### Beneficiary quotes
Direct feedback from program participants:
- **Success stories**: Quotes showing positive program impact
- **Challenges faced**: Feedback about barriers or difficulties
- **Program feedback**: Suggestions for improvement
- **Outcome evidence**: Quotes demonstrating achieved outcomes

## Adding annotations

1. **Open a KPI's detailed view** by selecting its card from the dashboard.

A screenshot showing the annotations section in a KPI detail view will be added here.

2. **Navigate to a specific time period** in the chart or data table.

3. **Select "Add Annotation"** for that period.

4. **Choose annotation type**:
   - **Note**: For general context and explanations
   - **Beneficiary Quote**: For direct beneficiary feedback

5. **Write your annotation**:
   - Keep notes concise but informative
   - Include specific details about interventions or changes
   - For quotes, include attribution if appropriate (while respecting privacy)

6. **Select "Save Annotation"** to add it to the KPI.

## Annotation best practices

### For notes
- **Be specific**: "Increased outreach in rural areas" vs "More outreach"
- **Include dates**: When did interventions start or external events occur?
- **Quantify when possible**: "Added 3 new staff members" vs "Added staff"
- **Link to actions**: What was done in response to performance changes?

### For beneficiary quotes
- **Respect privacy**: Use initials or program participant numbers instead of full names
- **Seek consent**: Ensure participants agree to their feedback being recorded
- **Provide context**: Brief background on the participant or situation
- **Balance perspectives**: Include both positive feedback and challenges

### Timing annotations
- **Real-time**: Add annotations as events happen for accurate context
- **Retrospective**: Review KPIs monthly to add missing context
- **Collaborative**: Involve program staff who have direct beneficiary contact

## Examples of effective annotations

### Intervention documentation
> **Note (March 2024)**: Launched mobile health clinic serving 3 remote villages. Expected to increase beneficiaries reached by 40% starting April 2024.

### External factor explanation
> **Note (December 2023)**: Seasonal floods disrupted program delivery. Road access limited until January 2024. Temporary mobile units deployed.

### Beneficiary impact evidence
> **Quote (June 2024)**: "The digital literacy training helped me start selling my crafts online. I've doubled my monthly income." - Participant P-47, Women's Empowerment Program

### Data quality context
> **Note (September 2024)**: Data collection system upgrade caused 1-week reporting delay. October figures include catch-up data from late September.

## Managing annotations

### Editing annotations
1. **Find the annotation** in the KPI detail view
2. **Select the edit icon** next to the annotation
3. **Make your changes** and save

### Deleting annotations
1. **Locate the annotation** to remove
2. **Select the delete icon**
3. **Confirm deletion** - this cannot be undone

:::warning
Deleting annotations is permanent and removes valuable context from your KPI history. Consider editing instead of deleting when information needs updating.
:::

## Using annotations for reporting

### Monthly reports
- **Review annotations** from the past month
- **Summarize key insights** for stakeholders
- **Highlight beneficiary feedback** that demonstrates impact
- **Document lessons learned** for future program planning

### Quarterly analysis
- **Look for patterns** in annotations across KPIs
- **Identify successful interventions** mentioned in notes
- **Track beneficiary sentiment** over time
- **Plan future improvements** based on documented challenges

### Annual evaluation
- **Compile beneficiary quotes** showing program impact
- **Document major interventions** and their effects
- **Analyze external factors** that influenced performance
- **Create narrative reports** combining data and context

## Integration with other features

### Charts and dashboards
Annotations added to KPIs provide context when the same metrics are used in charts and dashboards.

### Reports
Include KPI annotations in generated reports to provide rich context alongside performance data.

### Team collaboration
Share annotated KPIs with team members to ensure everyone understands performance context and contributing factors.
