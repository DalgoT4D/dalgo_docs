# KPI Annotations

**Add context and stories to your KPI data with annotations.**

KPI annotations allow you to capture important context, insights, and beneficiary voices alongside your performance data. This creates a rich timeline that combines quantitative metrics with qualitative stories.

## What are annotations?

Annotations are time-stamped entries linked to specific KPI periods. They help you:
- Explain unusual performance variations
- Document program changes or external factors
- Capture beneficiary quotes and success stories
- Provide context for stakeholders and funders

## Viewing the annotation timeline

Click any KPI card to open the detail drawer, which shows:
- **KPI chart**: Visual performance over time
- **Annotation timeline**: Chronological list of entries
- **Period selector**: Filter annotations by time period

## Types of annotations

### Comments
Internal notes and observations from your team:
- "Program enrollment was low due to monsoon flooding"
- "New training module introduced this quarter"
- "Staff capacity increased with additional hire"

### Beneficiary quotes
Direct voices from the people you serve:
- "The vocational training helped me start my own business" - Maria, Microfinance participant
- "My children are now attending school regularly thanks to the scholarship" - Priya, Education beneficiary
- "The health clinic saved my grandmother's life" - Abdul, Healthcare program

## Adding annotations

1. **Open the KPI detail drawer** by clicking any KPI card
2. **Select the time period** you want to annotate
3. **Choose annotation type**:
   - **Comment**: Internal team observations
   - **Beneficiary quote**: Direct beneficiary voices
4. **Write your annotation**
5. **Select Save** to add it to the timeline

### Attribution for beneficiary quotes
When adding beneficiary quotes:
- **Name**: First name or initials (respect privacy)
- **Program context**: Which program or service
- **Location** (optional): General area if relevant
- **Permission**: Ensure you have consent to share the quote

## Organizing annotations by period

Annotations are grouped by the KPI's time grain:
- **Monthly KPIs**: Annotations grouped by month
- **Quarterly KPIs**: Annotations grouped by quarter
- **Annual KPIs**: Annotations grouped by year

This makes it easy to understand what happened during specific performance periods and helps you identify patterns over time.

## Using annotations in reports

Annotations appear in exported KPI reports and can be shared with:
- **Funders**: Provide context behind performance numbers
- **Board members**: Show impact stories alongside metrics
- **Partners**: Share lessons learned and program insights
- **Beneficiaries**: Celebrate achievements and progress

## Best practices

### Regular annotation habits
- Add annotations during monthly/quarterly reviews
- Capture context immediately while events are fresh
- Include both challenges and successes

### Writing effective annotations
- Be specific: "10% increase due to partnership with local school" vs. "increase"
- Include numbers when relevant: "Trained 25 new volunteers"
- Focus on actionable insights for future planning

### Beneficiary quote guidelines
- Always get proper consent before recording quotes
- Use respectful language and authentic voices
- Include enough context to make the quote meaningful
- Protect privacy while maintaining authenticity
