# KPI Annotations

Add context to your KPIs with timeline annotations—internal comments for your team and beneficiary quotes that tell the story behind the numbers.

## What are Annotations?

Annotations let you capture the "why" behind KPI changes:
- **Comments**: Internal notes explaining trends, program changes, or external factors
- **Beneficiary Quotes**: Direct feedback from people you serve, with proper attribution
- **Auto-snapshots**: Each annotation captures the KPI's value and status at that moment
- **Timeline view**: See the full story of your KPI over time

## Adding Annotations

### 1. Open KPI Detail View

From the KPI page, click any KPI card to open the detail drawer.

### 2. Find the Annotations Section

Scroll down past the trend chart to see the **Annotations** timeline.

### 3. Click "Add Entry"

This opens the annotation form at the top of the timeline.

### 4. Choose Entry Type

#### Comment
For internal team notes:
- Program updates
- External factors affecting performance
- Methodology changes
- Strategic decisions

#### Beneficiary Quote  
For direct feedback from program participants:
- Success stories
- Challenges faced
- Suggestions for improvement
- Impact testimonials

### 5. Select Time Period

Choose which period this annotation relates to from the dropdown. The periods match your KPI's configured time grain (monthly, quarterly, etc.).

### 6. Add Content

#### For Comments
Write your internal note explaining what happened and why:

> "Enrollment peaked after the awareness campaign in March. School outreach + community events drove the 15% spike."

#### For Beneficiary Quotes
Add the quote and attribution:

**Quote**: 
> "My daughter enrolled because she finally had a school near our area. The new center made all the difference."

**Attribution**: `Beneficiary, Karnataka`

### 7. Save

When you save, the system automatically captures:
- **KPI value** at the time of the annotation
- **RAG status** (On Track/At Risk/Off Track)
- **Achievement percentage** 
- **Your name** as the author
- **Timestamp** when added

## Timeline View

### Organization
Annotations appear in reverse chronological order (newest first), grouped by time period.

### Each Entry Shows
- **Period label**: "Apr 2026" with value snapshot
- **Delta indicator**: "+337 vs Mar" (change from previous period)
- **Content**: Your comment or the beneficiary quote
- **Attribution**: For quotes, shows who said it
- **Metadata**: Author name and date added
- **Actions**: Edit (✎) and delete (🗑) on hover

### Multiple Entries Per Period
You can add multiple annotations to the same period—useful when several things influence a KPI change.

## Editing and Managing Annotations

### Edit Annotations
- **Hover over any entry** to see edit (✎) and delete (🗑) icons
- **Click edit** to modify the content
- **Period and value snapshot** cannot be changed (they're historical records)
- **Author and timestamp** remain unchanged

### Delete Annotations  
- **Click delete (🗑)** for inline confirmation
- **Permanent action**: Cannot be undone
- **Permissions**: Only the author or users with edit permissions can delete

### Ownership
- **Author visibility**: You can always edit/delete your own annotations
- **Permission-based**: Users with KPI edit permissions can modify any annotation
- **Audit trail**: Original author and timestamp are preserved even after edits

## Best Practices

### Writing Effective Comments

#### Be Specific
❌ "Good month"  
✅ "Enrollment peaked after the March awareness campaign. School outreach + community events drove the 15% increase."

#### Include Context
- What changed in your program?
- External factors affecting performance?  
- Strategic decisions made?
- Seasonal patterns?

#### Note Methodology Changes
- Data collection improvements
- Definition updates
- System migrations
- Process changes

### Capturing Beneficiary Quotes

#### Attribution Guidelines
- **Respect privacy**: Use "Beneficiary, [Location]" instead of full names
- **Be specific enough**: Include relevant context like location, program, or demographic
- **Stay consistent**: Use the same attribution format across your organization

#### Quote Selection
- **Authentic voices**: Use direct quotes, not paraphrases
- **Representative stories**: Choose quotes that reflect broader experiences
- **Balanced perspective**: Include both success stories and challenges
- **Impact focus**: Prioritize quotes that demonstrate program effects

### Timeline Maintenance

#### Regular Updates
- **Monthly check-ins**: Add context for significant changes
- **Quarterly reviews**: Explain broader trends and strategic shifts  
- **Event-driven**: Document major program changes, crises, or opportunities

#### Quality Control
- **Review old entries**: Ensure annotations remain accurate and relevant
- **Update attributions**: Refresh beneficiary quotes periodically
- **Remove outdated notes**: Delete annotations that are no longer meaningful

## Common Use Cases

### Program Changes
```
Period: Mar 2026
Value: 2,847 (+15% vs Feb)

"Launched new community outreach strategy this month. 
Local leaders now directly involved in enrollment drives, 
which explains the significant uptick in registrations."

— Priya, Program Manager
```

### External Factors
```
Period: Jan 2026  
Value: 2,510 (-4% vs Dec)

"Post-holiday dip as expected. Families traditionally 
delay enrollments until after winter festivals. 
February should show recovery."

— Noopur, M&E Coordinator
```

### Beneficiary Feedback
```
Period: Apr 2026
Value: 2,847

"My daughter enrolled because she finally had a school 
near our area. The transport was the biggest barrier 
before the new center opened."

— Beneficiary, Rural Karnataka
Added by Priya, Field Coordinator
```

### Methodology Updates
```
Period: Feb 2026
Value: 2,640

"Updated enrollment definition to include pre-registered 
students who completed orientation. This accounts for 
the 8% increase from revised counting methodology."

— Data Team
```

## Integration with Reports

### Report Snapshots
When you create report snapshots:
- **Full timeline** is preserved in the snapshot
- **Comments and quotes** remain visible to report viewers  
- **Attribution** is maintained for transparency
- **Historical context** helps stakeholders understand trends

### Stakeholder Updates
Use annotations to:
- **Explain performance** to board members
- **Share beneficiary stories** with funders
- **Document lessons learned** for program improvement
- **Provide accountability** through transparent reporting
